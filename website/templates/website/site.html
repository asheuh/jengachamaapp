Why you need to take care of your health?

For decades, people have been advised to eat a low-fat diet with carbs making up 50–60% of calories. Surprisingly, this advice was extended to include people with type 2 diabetes — who cannot tolerate a lot of easily digestible carbs, like sugar and refined starch. People with type 2 diabetes are resistant to insulin and any carbs they eat will cause a big rise in blood sugar levels. For this reason, they need to take blood-sugar-lowering drugs to bring their levels down. If anyone benefits from a low-carb diet, it is people with diabetes. In one study, following a low-carb diet for only 6 months allowed 95.2% of participants to reduce or eliminate their blood sugar medication
